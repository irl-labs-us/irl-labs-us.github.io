The IRL mental model

IRL is built around a few simple, opinionated concepts:

◎

Social Circles

Organize people by shared interests and context (“art friends”, “pick-up soccer”, “neighbors”), not just follower counts.

◈

Scenes

A flexible term for anything from ticketed shows to spontaneous “I’m at Sea Witch, come hang” broadcasts.

∞

Archetypes & Personas

Lightweight personas that tailor places, scenes, and people suggestions—without turning your life into “content.”

❤

Safety by design

Privacy-respecting presence, layered blocking/reporting, and geofence-aware features that never feel like surveillance.

How IRL works

Designed for ultra-low friction: you set intent, IRL quietly matches you with people, places, and scenes that fit.

1. Set intent

Choose what you’re here for

Deepen existing friendships, meet new people, discover places, date, or some mix. IRL tailors everything from there.

2. Build Social Circles

Interest-based, not clout-based

Add connections into circles like Art & Culture, Nightlife, Neighborhood, Sports & Play— with Dunbar-inspired caps to keep things human-sized.

3. Discover Scenes & Places

Your kind of “out”

A map-centric view of nearby scenes and venues, adapted to your archetype, personas, and subtle signals like “want to go” lists and micro-reactions.

4. Connect, safely

Broadcast when it matters

Send precise, scoped signals—“I’m open to hang”, “Working from this café”—to exactly the circles you choose, with safe-mode and privacy controls built-in.

ME Me

Profile & presence
  • • Minimal bio: photo, name, username, short “about.”
  • • Intent & status: fun, context-rich states like “Down for cozy drinks” or “Building in the corner booth.”
  • • Persona switcher: e.g. “On-shift bartender” vs “Off-duty social mode.”

◎ People & Social Circles

Dunbar-aware graph
  • • Interest-based circles (art, nightlife, wellness, etc.).
  • • Soft caps (e.g. 150 per persona) with gentle pruning suggestions instead of hoarding.
  • • Burner QR codes + invite tokens to add people nearby without oversharing your graph.

◎ Places & Scenes

Map-first view
  • • Map as home base (powered by Google Maps / Places).
  • • Import or add your own favorite spots, tag them by vibe (chill, creative, romantic, etc.).
  • • Create, save, or join Scenes: shows, dinners, co-working hangouts, spontaneous meetups.

Safety, privacy, and trust by default

IRL is built on “do no harm” principles: you control what you share, with whom, and when—backed by clear, enforceable safety rails.

Safe mode & location controls

  • • Location sharing options: Always off, While using (recommended), Always on (opt-in).
  • • Geofencing is silent—no public “you’re outside the zone” tells, and no precise trails.
  • • Safe Mode presets: conservative defaults that reduce reachability, hide certain signals, and limit discovery when needed.
  • • Data minimization: store only what’s necessary to make IRL work—not a location history someone could weaponize.

Layered blocking & moderation

  • • Hide & mute: they disappear from your broadcasts; you fade from theirs, but profiles remain separate.
  • • Remove connection: they drop from your circles and can’t see your scoped broadcasts.
  • • Block & report: hard boundary. If someone is blocked or reported enough times, their account is suspended and reviewed by moderators.
  • • All actions are designed to reduce social fallout—no harsh public signals, just better boundaries.

Loyalty & presence (for venues)

  • • Optional loyalty “stamps” that reward real visits without extra work for businesses.
  • • Configurable presence timer (e.g. 5+ minutes inside a geofence) before a visit can be claimed.
  • • One visit credit per day per venue to reduce abuse.
  • • Optional QR/NFC stickers as a low-lift “proof of presence” for higher-value rewards.

Product philosophy

  • • No infinite feeds, no algorithmic outrage loops.
  • • Everything orients around today’s possible connections, not “someday” fame.
  • • Designed to be multi-generational: language, flows, and visuals work for 20-somethings and 60-somethings alike.
  • • Revenue aligned with user value (e.g. premium connection limits, place tools, and ethical partnerships—not selling attention exhaust).

Build phases

A product-forward, experiment-heavy approach that respects both the technical stack and the social dynamics we’re trying to serve.

Phase 0 – Concept & Design

  • • Define archetypes & personas.
  • • Map key journeys: connect, explore, meet up.
  • • Low-fi wireframes → clickable prototypes.
  • • Validate with small, trusted groups.

Phase 1 – Web MVP & Private Beta

  • • Web app with Me, People, and Places & Scenes.
  • • Supabase-backed auth & data with privacy-first schema.
  • • Basic presence signals & scoped broadcasts.
  • • Early venue tools (loyalty, scene creation) with a handful of launch partners.

Phase 2+ – Mobile, Growth & Monetization

  • • Native apps with richer presence & notifications.
  • • Smarter matching using social graph, archetypes, and place tags.
  • • Premium tiers (more personas, advanced filters, venue tooling).
  • • City-by-city rollout with curated local networks.
